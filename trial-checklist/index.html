The arbitrage betting software trial checklist

A free trial answers the only question that matters — does this tool produce enough opportunities you can act on to justify its price? This checklist turns a trial into a measurement instead of a vibe.

Before you start the trial

  • ☐ List the bookmakers where you hold usable, unrestricted accounts. This list — not the tool's global count — is your real coverage.
  • ☐ Set your test bankroll and per-bet cap, separate from living money (bankroll guide).
  • ☐ Screenshot the tool's public pricing page with today's date, so you can spot changes at renewal time.
  • ☐ Read the cancellation terms before entering any payment details.
  • ☐ Create a simple log: date, market, bookmakers, odds quoted, odds actually available, stake, outcome, fees.

Day 1–2: configuration

  • ☐ Filter the bookmaker list to only the books you can use. Screenshot it, with the date.
  • ☐ Set minimum-margin and sport filters to something realistic, not the promotional defaults.
  • ☐ Verify the odds format and stake calculator against our independent arbitrage calculator — the numbers must agree.

The core test week

  • ☐ Run at least five consecutive ordinary days, not just a big sports weekend.
  • ☐ For every alert you would have taken: open both bookmakers and record whether the quoted odds were actually available. This is the odds-freshness test that separates real scanners from stale feeds.
  • ☐ Count actionable opportunities per day — alerts where both legs were placeable at your books, at your stakes.
  • ☐ Note alert-to-action time. If prices are gone before you can react, the volume number is fiction (see common mistakes).
  • ☐ Check market-rules pairs on anything you place — void-rule mismatches are the classic silent killer.

Before you pay

  • ☐ Multiply your measured actionable margin by realistic monthly volume. Does it clear the subscription cost with room to spare?
  • ☐ Confirm what the renewal price is (not the promotional price) and how billing currency converts to yours.
  • ☐ Re-read your log. Decide on the data, not on how the dashboard made you feel.

Red flags that mean walk away

  • Marketing that promises guaranteed or risk-free returns — no honest operator in this space promises that.
  • No way to filter to your country's bookmakers, or a coverage list the support team won't confirm in writing.
  • Odds that consistently fail the freshness spot-check.
  • Pressure pricing: discounts that expire in hours, or cancellation flows designed to be hard.
