Bookmaker limits and account restrictions

Every arbitrage strategy has the same expiry mechanism: bookmakers restrict accounts that consistently take the wrong prices. Anyone selling you arbitrage software without mentioning this is not being straight with you.

Why bookmakers limit

Retail bookmakers price markets with a margin and manage risk by watching who beats their prices. An account that repeatedly bets stale or outlying odds — the exact behaviour arbitrage requires — is a cost, and most bookmakers' terms let them cap stakes or close accounts at their discretion. It is commercial, not personal, and it is legal in most jurisdictions.

How arbitrage bettors get flagged

  • Betting odd stake amounts (the classic $87.43 arb split — round your stakes).
  • Only betting when a price is an outlier against the market.
  • Betting obscure markets and minor leagues with sudden precision.
  • Depositing, betting maximum limits immediately, and withdrawing fast.
  • Never betting recreationally — no favourites, no accumulators, ever.

What actually happens

Restrictions usually arrive as stake limits ("maximum bet: $12.50") rather than closures. Winnings from settled bets are generally paid; the account just becomes commercially useless. Timelines vary enormously by bookmaker and country — we will not quote average account lifespans because we have not measured them, and most numbers you see quoted elsewhere are folklore.

What software can and cannot do

Scanners find the opportunities; they cannot make a bookmaker keep taking your bets. Some tools reduce the footprint — rounding stakes, filtering to high-liquidity markets, spreading volume — and exchanges and betting brokers are structurally different because their business model profits from volume rather than losing customers. Which tools support exchanges and brokers is a key comparison point in our software hub and in the RebelBetting review.

What not to do

Opening accounts in other people's names or evading identity verification to escape limits breaches bookmaker terms and can cross into fraud in some jurisdictions. Treat limits as a cost of the strategy, not a problem to engineer around. Plan for them instead: spread bankroll across books (see bankroll management), and consider value betting as the longer-lived second phase.
