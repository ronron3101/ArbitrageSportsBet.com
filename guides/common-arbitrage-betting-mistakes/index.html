Common arbitrage betting mistakes

A surebet is only sure if every assumption behind it holds. These are the assumptions beginners break most often.

1. Assuming markets are identical across bookmakers

The most expensive mistake. "Draw no bet" vs "double chance", overtime included vs excluded, player props settled by different data providers — two markets with similar names can settle differently, and then you don't have an arb, you have two uncorrelated bets. Read the market rules at both bookmakers until this check is reflexive.

2. Betting palpable errors

If an arb margin looks too good — double digits on a mainstream market — one bookmaker has probably made a pricing error, and most terms let them void such bets. The void kills one leg and leaves you fully exposed on the other. Big margins deserve suspicion, not bigger stakes.

3. Rule differences on voids

Even without palps: a postponed match, an abandoned game, a withdrawn player. Bookmakers differ on when bets stand, push or void. If one leg voids and the other stands, your hedge is gone. Know each bookmaker's policy for the sports you bet.

4. Slow or partial execution

Odds move. Place the leg at the fragile price (usually the outlier that created the arb) first; if you get caught halfway, the hedge calculator tells you the least-bad exit. Speed matters more in live markets — treat live arbing as an advanced skill, not a starting point.

5. Ignoring costs on a thin edge

A 2% margin shrinks fast under currency spreads, deposit/withdrawal fees, and exchange commission. Count them per bet honestly — the bankroll guide covers the operational side.

6. Obvious arb footprints

Unrounded stakes and outlier-only betting get accounts limited quickly. See bookmaker limits for what flags accounts and what to do about it — and what never to do.

7. Subscribing before measuring

Paying for a scanner before checking you can act on its alerts — at your bookmakers, in your country, at your stakes — is buying the answer before reading the question. Free trials exist; use them with our trial checklist and the comparison hub.

8. No records

Without a log of every bet — odds taken, stake, result, fees — you cannot distinguish edge from luck or spot which bookmaker is quietly costing you money. A spreadsheet from bet one is non-negotiable.
